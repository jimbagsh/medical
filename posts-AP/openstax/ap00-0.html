Preface

Welcome to Anatomy and Physiology for Non-native English Speakers. This online textbook was created to help non-native English healthcare professionals, like doctors, nurses and dentists, to improve their medical English.

The source for the materials is an online textbook called "Anatomy and Physiology" by OpenStax.com, an online university textbook provider of free peer-reviewed books. The text grammar has been made easier but still helps the viewer learn new medical English terms in the context of medical information.


How to Use these Lessons

In every lesson, you will see words 'highlighted' like this word. If you hover over the word, a popup box will appear with the definition of that word. Try it! If you click the word, then your device will say the word or phrase. Go ahead and try it!

At the end of every lesson, you will also find a Glossary of the new words from the lesson, complete with definition and if you click the 'play' button next to the word, you can hear the word out loud. The other tabs give the English-to-Mongolian and the English-to-Russian translations.


About Medical.Anglihel.com

We try to bridge the gap between the medical knowledge doctors and nurses already have with the English they need to know better.

Our goal is to help doctors, nurses and other healthcare professionals who want to improve their medical English by using online textbooks, medical terminology lessons, and doctor-patient conversations.

About Anatomy and Physiology

These lessons follow to the material and sequence used by most two-semester courses in the United States. The choices for this textbook were made with the guidance of hundreds of faculty. OpenStax.org tried very hard to make the text meaningful and memorable to students. It is hoped that they can use their new knowledge that will help their future studies.


Unit 1: Levels of Organization

Chapters 1–4 gives a basic understanding of human anatomy and physiology, including its language, the levels of organization, and the basics of chemistry and cell biology. These chapters provide a foundation for the more study of the body. They also focus on how the body’s regions, important chemicals, and cells maintain homeostasis.

   Chapter 1 An Introduction to the Human Body
   Chapter 2 The Chemical Level of Organization
   Chapter 3 The Cellular Level of Organization
   Chapter 4 The Tissue Level of Organization


Unit 2: Support and Movement

In Chapters 5–11, the lessons explore the skin, the largest organ of the body, and examine the body’s skeletal and muscular systems. This unit is the first to walk through specific systems of the body. It maintains a focus on homeostasis as well as those diseases and conditions that can disrupt it.

   Chapter 5 The Integumentary System
   Chapter 6 Bone and Skeletal Tissue
   Chapter 7 The Axial Skeleton
   Chapter 8 The Appendicular Skeleton
   Chapter 9 Joints
   Chapter 10 Muscle Tissue
   Chapter 11 The Muscular System


Unit 3: Regulation, Integration, and Control

Chapters 12–17 answer questions about nervous and endocrine system control and regulation. The chapter on the neurological examination offers a approach to understanding nervous system function using five diagnostic tests.

   Chapter 12 Introduction to the Nervous System
   Chapter 13 The Anatomy of the Nervous System
   Chapter 14 The Somatic Nervous System
   Chapter 15 The Autonomic Nervous System
   Chapter 16 The Neurological Exam
   Chapter 17 The Endocrine System


Unit 4: Fluids and Transport

In Chapters 18–21, examine the principal means of transport for materials needed to support the human body, regulate its internal environment, and provide protection.

   Chapter 18 Blood
   Chapter 19 The Cardiovascular System: The Heart
   Chapter 20 The Cardiovascular System: Blood Vessels and Circulation
   Chapter 21 The Lymphatic System and Immunity


Unit 5: Energy, Maintenance, and Environmental Exchange

In Chapters 22–26, discover the interaction between body systems and the outside environment for the exchange of materials, the capture of energy, the release of waste, and the maintenance of the internal systems that regulate the exchange.

   Chapter 22 The Respiratory System
   Chapter 23 The Digestive System
   Chapter 24 Nutrition and Metabolism
   Chapter 25 The Urinary System
   Chapter 26 Fluid, Electrolyte, and Acid–Base Balance


Unit 6: Human Development and the Continuity of Life

The closing chapters examine the male and female reproductive systems, describe the process of human development and the different stages of pregnancy, and end with a review of the mechanisms of inheritance.

   Chapter 27 The Reproductive System
   Chapter 28 Development and Genetic Inheritance
