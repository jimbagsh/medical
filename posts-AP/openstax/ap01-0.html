Chapter 1: Introduction to the Human Body
Introduction

Knowledge in anatomy and physiology is needed for any career in the health professions.
(credit: Bryan Mason/flickr)

The knowledge you gain in this course will serve you well in many aspects of your life. An understanding of anatomy and physiology is is fundamental to any career in the health professions. But it can also benefit your own health. Being familiar with the body can help you make healthful choices and help you to take action when ill. Your knowledge will help you understand health news and help you understand diseases. At some time, everyone will have a problem with some part of his or her body. And your knowledge can help you to be a better parent, spouse, partner, friend, colleague, or caregiver.

This chapter begins with an overview of anatomy and physiology and a preview of the body regions and functions. It then covers the characteristics of life and how the body works to maintain stable conditions. It introduces a set of standard terms for body structures and for planes and positions in the body. It ends with examples of medical imaging used to see inside the living body.
